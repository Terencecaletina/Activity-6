    10 Non-fiction Books with Definition List

    1. Behind the Beautiful Forevers: Life, Death, and Hope in a Mumbai Undercity by Katherine Boo:
      A Pulitzer Prize-winning account of the lives of the residents of a slum in Mumbai, India, and their struggles with poverty, corruption, and inequality. (Published in 2012)


    2. Freakonomics: A Rogue Economist Explores the Hidden Side of Everything by Steven D. Levitt and Stephen J. Dubner:
      A best-selling book that applies economic principles to various social phenomena, such as crime, cheating, parenting, and sports, and reveals surprising connections and insights. (Published in 2005)


    3. The Emperor of All Maladies: A Biography of Cancer by Siddhartha Mukherjee:
      A National Book Award-winning book that traces the history of cancer, from its ancient origins to its modern treatments, and explores the scientific, cultural, and personal aspects of the disease. (Published in 2010)


    4. The Sixth Extinction: An Unnatural History by Elizabeth Kolbert:
      A Pulitzer Prize-winning book that examines the current mass extinction event caused by human activity, and its consequences for the planet and its biodiversity. (Published in 2014)


    5. How to Survive a Plague by David France:
      A definitive history of the AIDS epidemic in the United States, and the activism and advocacy that led to the development of life-saving drugs and policies. (Published in 2016)


    6. Crying in H Mart by Michelle Zauner:
      A memoir by the indie rock musician and author, who reflects on her Korean American identity, her relationship with her mother, and her grief after her mother’s death from cancer. (Published in 2021)


    7. Invisible Child by Andrea Elliott:
      A book based on the Pulitzer Prize-winning series of articles by the New York Times reporter, who follows the life of a homeless girl in Brooklyn and exposes the systemic failures that trap millions of Americans in poverty. (Published in 2021)


    8. The Revolutionary by Stacy Schiff:
      A biography of Benjamin Franklin, one of the founding fathers of the United States, who was a polymath, diplomat, inventor, and leader of the American Revolution. (Published in 2023)


    9. South to America by Imani Perry:
      A book that explores the history and culture of the Black South, and its influence on the African American experience and identity. (Published in 2023)


    10. The Code Breaker: Jennifer Doudna, Gene Editing, and the Future of the Human Race by Walter Isaacson:
      A biography of the Nobel Prize-winning scientist Jennifer Doudna, who co-discovered the revolutionary gene-editing technology CRISPR, and explores the ethical and social implications of her work. (Published in 2021)
